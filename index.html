Is this a page about pantomimes? Oh no it isn't!

Oh yet it is.

Pantomime is a type of musical comedy aimed at the entire family. Confusingly, it has nothing to do with miming. It's a british genre, performed in a few other countries, such as:

  • Ireland
  • Australia
  • Canada
  • USA
  • Andorra
  • Malta
  • Jamaica
  • Switzerland

Pantomime conventions

Panto conventions have changed over the years, and none are mandatory, but here are some of the common tropes of the genre:

  1. The lead boy is often played by a young woman
  2. There is a pantomime dame, usually the principal boy's mother, played by a man in drag who makes horrible innuendos
  3. On that note, lots of innuendos and puns
  4. Audience participation, particularly singing along, shouting He's behind you, arguing with characters, and awwwing whenever something remotely sad happens
  5. Singing and dancing numbers with simple, cheesy choreography, and pop songs rewritten to match the plot of the show
  6. A pair of people in an animal costume, usually a horse of cow
  7. Good characters entering from the right, bad characters entering from the left
